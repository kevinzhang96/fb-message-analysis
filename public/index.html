Hello there.

Your friends are waiting.

We spend a hell of a lot of time these days connected to the internet. It's no surprise, either; the internet has enabled us to effectively stay in contact with the ones for longer, faster, and arguably better with the implementation of multimedia messages. From AIM messages to modern email, there are a vast multitude of channels to plug in and type away. And with the ease of access and speed of messages being sent, the volume of information captured in our online communications is undoubtedly unparalleled.

We want to hack that information. Using personal data exported from Facebook, the world's largest social network and the host of Messenger, an instant messaging application, we parsed through the raw data, organized it, and analyzed it. We've produced the results here in a format that's easy to consume and allows you to explore aspects of behavior otherwise hidden.

These are our results. These are our real friends.
